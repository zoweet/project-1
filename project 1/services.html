Swim Levels

Parent Tot: Helps young children become comfortable in and around the water so they are ready to learn to swim at the next level.

  • Parent or caregiver accompanies child
  • Typically 6 months to 2 years of age
  • Children who are not toilet-trained will be required to wear special swim diaper*

    Tadpoles (Level 1): Introduction to Water Skills

  • Helps students feel comfortable in the water and to enjoy water safety.
  • Typically 3 to 5 years old.
  • Children who are not toilet-trained will be required to wear special swim diaper*

    • Minnows (Level 2): Fundamental Aquatic Skills

  • Gives students success with fundamental skills.
  • Typically 4 to 6 years old.

    • Stingrays (Level 3): Stroke Development

  • Builds on the skills of Level 2 by providing additional guided practice.
  • Typically 4 to 6 years old.

    • Dolphins (Level 4): Stroke Improvement

  • Develops confidence in the strokes previously learned and to improve other aquatic skills.

    • Sharks (Level 5): Stroke Refinement

  • Provides further coordination and refinement of strokes.
  • Typically 6 to 10 years old.
